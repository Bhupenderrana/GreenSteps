Did You Know?

Deforestation is occurring at an alarming rate. According to the United Nations Food and Agriculture Organization, the world's forests have decreased by approximately 178 million hectares since 1990. Deforestation has many negative impacts on the environment, including the loss of habitat for numerous plant and animal species, as well as contributing to climate change by releasing large amounts of carbon dioxide into the atmosphere. In addition, deforestation can lead to soil erosion, water pollution, and disruption of local communities who rely on forests for their livelihoods.
Did you know that approximately 8 million metric tons of plastic waste end up in the world's oceans every year? This plastic waste not only pollutes the ocean, but it also harms marine life, disrupts entire ecosystems, and poses a risk to human health. Plastic waste can also take hundreds of years to decompose, meaning that it will continue to cause harm for generations to come.
According to a report by the United Nations, animal agriculture is responsible for more greenhouse gas emissions than all the world's transportation combined. This includes emissions from livestock, as well as emissions from the production and transportation of feed and other inputs. Therefore, reducing our meat consumption or switching to plant-based diets can have a significant impact on reducing greenhouse gas emissions and helping to mitigate climate change.
Soil pollution is harmful substances or chemicals in soil that can have negative impacts on plant, animal and human health. It's caused by industrial activities, mining, waste disposal, and agricultural practices. Soil pollution can lead to a range of environmental and public health issues, but it can be reduced by implementing measures to clean up contaminated sites, reducing the use of chemicals in agriculture, and supporting sustainable farming practices such as organic farming and crop rotation.
